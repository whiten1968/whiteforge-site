HoroCalc

Five precision calculators for mechanical watch and clock work. Whether you’re sourcing a replacement mainspring, verifying a gear train, or adjusting a pendulum, HoroCalc gives you the number you need — instantly and on your phone, not buried in a spreadsheet.

£4.99 · iPhone · iOS 17 or later · Coming soon to the App Store

Five calculators for the workbench

  • Mainspring Sizer

    Enter the barrel bore, arbor diameter, and barrel height and HoroCalc calculates the correct spring length, width, and thickness using the standard ⅓ barrel-fill rule. If you already know the spring thickness, enter it and the calculation becomes exact. Useful for sourcing a replacement when the original spring is missing or damaged.

  • Beat Rate

    Select from the six most common frequencies — 18,000, 21,600, 25,200, 28,800, 36,000, or 72,000 BPH — or enter any value. Instantly shows the balance frequency in Hz, the beat interval in milliseconds, and the exact period. Enter a daily rate error to see the deviation in parts per million and whether the watch is running fast or slow.

  • Pendulum

    Works in both directions: give it a pendulum length and it calculates the beat rate; give it a target beat rate and it gives you the length to cut the rod. The bob adjustment section takes a measured daily rate error and tells you exactly how many millimetres to raise or lower the bob to correct it.

  • Gear Train

    Enter the tooth counts for the centre, third, fourth, and escape wheels and their pinions. HoroCalc calculates the resulting beat rate, balance frequency, beat interval, escape wheel speed, and fourth wheel RPM. The fourth wheel reading turns green when it hits 1.000 RPM — the correct rate for a watch with a seconds hand on the fourth wheel. A layout picker handles watch with centre seconds, watch with sub-seconds dial, and clock configurations.

  • Power Reserve

    Enter the barrel bore, arbor diameter, spring thickness, barrel rate, and a drive efficiency figure (typically 75–85%). HoroCalc calculates the maximum number of turns, the useful turns at that efficiency, and the resulting power reserve in hours — with a days figure for longer reserves.

Built for the bench

Every calculator opens with pre-populated example values so you can see a live result immediately. Inputs are numerical with decimal pads — no fumbling with the keyboard when your hands are covered in oil. Results are displayed in large, legible text that’s easy to read across the workbench.

HoroCalc works entirely offline. There are no accounts, no cloud sync, no advertising, and no analytics. Enter a number, get an answer.
